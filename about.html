// Two Become One

As far back as I can remember, technology is something that's always been a passion of mine. I was introduced to computers in my early school years and was lucky to have one in home growing up. It was incredible that I could use it to play games or to learn about something new using cds and floppy disks. Gaming is another one that has stuck by me right there with technology. The first game system I ever played was a Sega Genesis in 1999. As I kept up with trends through school and on my own at home, I eventually ended up getting a Gameboy Advance and one of the games that changed my life was Megaman Battle Network. I remember going into the Babbage's Game Store at our mall and looking through the used games and there it was. The sticker stuck out to me with the circuit lines giving it a feel like it was something futuristic. When I played Megaman Battle Network for the first time, I was hooked on the way their internet world worked. The idea that kids had their own webpages but took full responsibilty for the design and maintenance of it was so cool. The idea that you made money just doing various IT tasks online was beyond amazing. But the mind-blowing part of it all were the NetNavis. Digital beings that were multipurposed for communications, companionship, and even competitive play. Having a NetNavi was a pipe dream back then but its 2024 and technology grew. So did I. From the desire to get more involved with web design and development both as a hobby and a career, to the one of finding more like minded individuals in the ways that social media can't provide, something has been callng me to the web again and again and its this dream that we are on the cusp of that game being a reality. Hi, I'm Cory, a polymath of various skills and interests. This place I call Paradigm will be the home of that dream. I'll be displaying all sorts of works from handdrawn art to 3d gaming designs. Hopefully as this personal web grows, so will I and my dream of a more interactive internet.
